Alpha Roadmap

This site is a step by step prototype guide for employment.

This prototype will be designed with case managers and job seekers to meet job seeker needs of guidance and support for navigating public services during critical periods of unemployment. And model how to create simpler, user-centered multi-persona roadmaps.

This is a template that local employment specialist and case managers can use to customize for their independent job seeker to do list. It is not a real application: no data entered on these pages will be shared or stored.

Background

Building on the work of Workforce Development modernization, Code for America and the Municipality of Anchorage have been working to use the tools of technology and design to improve how the hardest to reach job seekers get access to critical services and resources for successful re-entry into the workforce.

Our team began by exploring the current state of job seeker services and delivery across local city job centers and nonprofits in an extensive qualitative user research field study. See the report of our findings.

From here, our team began working closely with local case managers to identify tools to help accelerate their service delivery modernization efforts.

About this prototype

This prototype demonstrates a user-centered enrollment model that leverages data sharing between agencies and case managers to minimize the burden on applicants in determining program eligibility and accessing governmental services in their journey of re-entry and re-employment.

In our research, we saw a common need from job seekers for guidance on overcoming personal challenges to re-employment. This was validated by a common tasks provided by servicers to refer non-eligible clients to an independent search of navigating public services and resources through inconsistent means. Thus, an online roadmap template which offices can customize to meet the needs of independent job seeker clients has been viewed as a desired solution through initial rounds of testing.

The alpha version of the prototype is designed to model how one or more applicants can use the tailored roadmap to guide them on the necessary things to do to become job-ready; particularly for re-entrants with low level computer skills.

Design principles for user-centered online roadmap to job-readiness

1. Design for real-time job readiness determination, managing job search expectations, and detailed instruction for agency interactions for accessing resources and needed documents.

Model how providers would collect data to verify against other government systems when possible.

2. Work to connect people to the programs they want and need, in as few steps as possible.

Give applicants the option to complete multiple tracks at once or to perform one track while informing them of other programs available to them.

3. Mobile-first responsive design.

Ensure that people can utilize tool on all devices by designing first for small screens and low bandwidth.

4. Plain language, warm voice, and motivating tone.

These programs are complicated and re-entry to employment is emotionally stressful. Do the hard work to keep the wording deadly simple. Use a conversational, encouraging tone and show people where they are in the process at all times.

5. Fewer prompts per screen.

Ask people questions in small bite-sized chunks, so as not to overwhelm with multiple complex questions at any single step. Always let people know where they are in the process.

6. Personalize and tailor applications.

Only show applicants information related to their specific circumstance.

What does “alpha” mean?

The alpha stage of product development is a short phase after a deep discovery in which teams quickly design, build, and test concepts with users.

The aim of the alpha phase is to:

  1. Show that project is technically feasible
  2. Build and test early versions of a product with real users
  3. Gather feedback from stakeholders and key partners to identify improvements for a beta release

In other words: this prototype is a work-in-progress... and we want your help making it better.
